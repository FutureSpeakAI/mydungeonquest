- The engine's 0.9 mock corpus templates carry em dashes (~9 of 24 turns with the director's intent riding). The engine is untouchable, so any game-side prose scan WILL convict the house's own fixtures.
- **Rule:** deterministic tiers (mock/fallback) fold at the SOURCE door in the game's DM wrapper — before the Art Director composes cues and before the validator seals ("born at zero"). Live tiers keep the whole teaching loop: pre-pass flag → mandatory revise → ship-door fold.
- **Why:** a deterministic tier cannot learn from a revise (the redraft is just the next template, often also dashed) — flagging it burns the revision budget on fixtures, breaks 0.9-era ledger pins (editor_calls/flags/verdict), and a post-chair fold desynchronizes `image_cue.moment` from the folded page (moment-mirror law). Folding pre-chair keeps every downstream invariant whole; the 0.9 eval bed's clean-draft premise survives with zero rig edits.
- **How to apply:** whole-turn fold (`dashFoldTurn`, one pinned source in voice.js) folds narration AND the cue's quoted moment in lockstep; captions compose from staging, not prose, so they never fold. Any future prose-law fold must ride the same seat.
- Calibration sweeps must attach the director's intent to the input — template selection shifts with intent; a no-intent sweep misses colliding turns (turn 7 looked clean without intent, dashed with it).
- Tells lexicon vs mock corpus: zero collisions (July 2026 sweep, with intent). The dash law was the only collider.
- First cut tried recutting the eval pins (sampling law moved to turn 14) — reverted as strictly worse: when a new content law convicts untouchable upstream fixtures, cure at the source door; don't recut the bed eval-by-eval (the recut surface cascades: sampling pins, flags pins, byte-determinism pins, moment mirrors).

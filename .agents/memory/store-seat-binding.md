---
name: Store seat-binding law
description: Courts may only join evidence the record itself seals on one row; empty seats are spoken, not thrown; new plate variants must be swept into every older court's filters.
---

**The rule.** A proving court may pair two pieces of stored evidence (a cue with a plate, a caption with bytes) ONLY where the record itself binds them — in MyDungeon.Quest, that means on ONE log row (papers stamp the hanging turn; even the paint salt cites the arrival row). Late-landing paints carry no commission link, so no deterministic cue↔plate join exists for them anywhere in the store.

**Why:** two courts invented a same-row join that "usually" held, then convicted a bust of failing a scene caption and a scene cue of missing a soul that hung on another row. The app never promised the pairing (premise law: enforce only promises the app makes).

**How to apply:**
- Build vision seats by resolving the row's own papers to a store entry and require the entry class the cue commissions (scene = variant null). Atelier mints (bust/sheet/dramatic/full-figure) answer to their own courts.
- When no lawful seat exists, that is an EMPTY SEAT, not a broken contract: speak a stand-down line (console) and return after the deterministic half has run. Reserve fail-closed throws for evidence that must exist by contract (e.g. the store itself missing).
- Kindred trap: when a store grows NEW plate variants, every older kind-blind court sweeps them by default — retarget each court's filters in the same task that adds the variant (same family as moved-surface-instruments).
- Geometry courts: settle the element's own animations (getAnimations().finished) before asserting rects — an entrance flight (scale 1.01) reads a hair wider than the glass and convicts an honest box.

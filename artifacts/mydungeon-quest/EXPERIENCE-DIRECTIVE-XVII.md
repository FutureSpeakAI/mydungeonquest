# EXPERIENCE DIRECTIVE XVII — THE BETA CUT

Task 60B. Closes at 1.0.1. The Series Preamble applies in full; this directive
extends the standing directives and yields to none of them. It is written
FIRST, before any code of the cut, per the standing directive-first law.
Built in three stages, each proven keyless before the next begins.

**THE BETA DEFINITION** (stated in one line, as ordered): beta is the free
local build behind invites, with cloud and billing arriving as their own
tasks after.

A founder playtest convicted seams the harness never walked: plates shipping
without their papers, captions sliced mid-word, validator notes surfacing as
player toasts, stale images standing in for the living moment, environments
and costumes drifting, a villain leaking into the Book before his
introduction, and dead air at the threshold of a new world. Every article
below exists because a real player met the seam it closes.

## ARTICLE 0 — THE ONE-ROAD LAW (governing)

**The law.** The application and the proving harness share the same pipeline
code. There is ONE road from sealed prose to rendered plate — cue seal,
validation, prompt composition, paint, attestation, render door — and both
the app and the courts walk it. Every player-visible plate must carry its
attested cue and caption or it cannot render. Reducer notes are ledger-only:
no note, violation, or repair detail may enter any render payload, and the
curtain of 0.9.0 extends over toasts. Fault injection joins the courts, so
the fallback branches are finally judged instead of trusted.

**Enforcement point.** A single pipeline module owned by the app and imported
by the harness — never mirrored, never copied (the one-seat law of mirrors).
The render door refuses paperless or captionless plates structurally: the
plate component cannot mount without its attestation. Render payload
assembly strips ledger fields by construction (a whitelist of what MAY
cross, never a blacklist of what may not).

**The gate.** The oneRoad gate (keyless, stage one) proves the shared module
and the door's refusals; G31's injected-fault legs and G32's harvested-DOM
sweep judge the road under fire.

## ARTICLE I — THE REFERENCE SHEET LAW

**The law.** Every carded subject earns exactly one composite reference
sheet: souls, places, species, and notable items — a notable item being a
named thing the tale features, not every ladle in the trove. A sheet is a
single image in a fixed grid with a fixed cell order: the face, the profile,
the full figure in current costume, and the defining detail. Sheets are
minted once at introduction, derived by edit-mode operations from the sealed
anchor rather than fresh rolls, with canonical cells at neutral pose, eye
level, and flat lighting; minting prompts carry the silence clause, so no
sheet bears labels or lettering of any kind. A sheet is attested by hash and
judged by the sheet court before first use. It is re-minted only when canon
lawfully changes through an operation, and the prior sheet remains attested
for every plate it grounded. Plates ground on the original sheet forever;
generations are never chained, so cumulative drift has no mechanism. A
directive-sanctioned re-anchor lever exists for a tale whose constancy
yellows climb; it defaults OFF and moves only by the owner's hand.

**Enforcement point.** The sheet mint rides the one road's edit-mode door;
the easel refuses to attach an unattested or court-unjudged sheet; the
attestation carries the sheet's hash, subject, and minting operation.

**The gate.** The sheet gate (keyless, stage one); G31's sheet court under
the standing instrument law; TOOTH 20 proves the court can convict a sheet
crossed against the wrong soul and a deliberately lettered sheet.

## ARTICLE II — THE SLOT LAW

**The law.** Reference budgets are pinned constants per provider; for the
Gemini painter they are FIVE identifiable subjects and FOURTEEN total
reference images per call. Selection is deterministic: the principal's sheet
always rides; the ground's place sheet always rides; remaining cue subjects
follow in cue order; one species sheet covers every instance of that
species; fixtures ride the place sheet and never spend a slot; items ride as
sealed canon text unless this turn's prose features a notable item, which
then earns its sheet a slot. Every attached sheet gets a binding line in the
prompt stating in plain words which subject it depicts, in deterministic
order, byte-stable. The validator enforces the identity ceiling on the cue
itself, and the Art Director overflows by direction — composing shots that
do not need every face — so the model is never asked to hold more likenesses
than the pinned budget.

**Enforcement point.** One deterministic slot selector inside the shared
pipeline module; the validator's ceiling sits on the cue at the door, not at
the easel.

**The gate.** The slot gate (keyless, stage one); G31's closure court counts
at the five-subject bar.

## ARTICLE III — THE FRESH PLATE LAW

**The law.** Every rendered plate is bound by attestation to the turn it
illustrates, and the render door refuses a plate without this-turn papers. A
failed paint yields an honest empty frame with its status named — never a
recycled image — and no code path may reuse a prior turn's plate as the
current turn's illustration. The Art Director's brief is a storyboard panel
of the sealed prose, under this shot grammar stated in general language: the
frame serves the moment's action; the angle serves the moment's power
(looking up at what looms, level with what converses, down at what lies
low); blocking places the principal where the prose places them; light obeys
the scene's stated hour and named sources; and no shot repeats the prior
plate's framing when the moment has moved.

**Enforcement point.** Turn-stamped attestation minted at cue seal, checked
at the render door by the shared module; the empty frame is a first-class
rendered state with its status named in plain speech.

**The gate.** The freshPlate gate (keyless, stage one); G31's fresh-plate
walk proves every feed plate carries this-turn papers, an injected stale
plate is refused with the empty frame shown honestly, and TOOTH 21 proves a
fresh-plate law that can see: the planted prior-turn plate must be refused
by the door AND flagged by the court.

## ARTICLE IV — THE PROP LAW

**The law.** An item may appear in a plate only if its holder stands in the
cue, it is a fixture of the current ground, or this turn's operations moved
it. This ends the wandering lantern and the ubiquitous bell at the door,
rather than at the easel.

**Enforcement point.** The validator, on the cue's item list — refusals are
named on the cue before any paint is asked.

**The gate.** The propLaw gate (keyless, stage one); G31's seeded lantern
renders only where the law allows it.

## ARTICLE V — THE HOUSE VOICE LAW

**The law.** The Dungeon Master writes like a fantasy author, not like a
language model. The craft canon, distilled: concrete nouns over abstractions;
active verbs over auxiliary scaffolding; the senses before the summary; the
scene entered late and left early; sentence music varied — short after long,
never three alike; character speech that belongs to one mouth; no
throat-clearing openers, no hedged weather-report prose, no inventory lists
wearing a paragraph's clothes. An exemplar shelf of public-domain passages
rides as known-good fixtures. A pinned lexicon of language-model tells
carries a density ceiling enforced in the Editor's deterministic pre-pass.
No in-copyright text enters the repository or any prompt; style is
inspiration, never imitation of any living author's protected expression.
THE DASH LAW is absolute and deterministic: ZERO em dashes in shipped
narration, enforced as a mandatory-revise flag in the pre-pass, and scanned
at zero across the mock corpus and every harvested page.

**Enforcement point.** The Editor's deterministic pre-pass (lexicon and dash
scans run before any judged look); the craft canon and exemplar shelf ride
the writer's prompt from one pinned source.

**The gate.** The houseVoice gate (keyless, stage two); TOOTH 10 extends — a
planted page dense with em dashes and tells must be flagged by the pre-pass
and judged revise with the dash law among its named reasons.

## ARTICLE VI — THE UNMET LAW

**The law.** The Book shows only souls the record has introduced; the unmet
render as absence. A villain can never leak into the codex before the tale
speaks him.

**Enforcement point.** The Book's shelf reads the record's introduction
ledger — presence in canon is not presence in the tale until the record says
so.

**The gate.** The unmet gate (keyless, stage three); G33 proves the villain
absent before his introduction and present after.

## ARTICLE VII — THE THRESHOLD LAW

**The law.** World genesis gets a branded loading screen with true progress
states named in plain speech and the house sigil standing. One config-gated
promo slot exists for the house's own word; it defaults OFF. No third-party
advertising surface exists in this cut — that question waits for the toll
design where it belongs. The First Word law is untouched: the prologue
request still outruns the paint.

**Enforcement point.** The genesis screen renders the pipeline's true stage
names, never invented percentages; the promo slot reads its config gate and
ships dark.

**The gate.** The threshold gate (keyless, stage three); G33 proves the
states appear in order on the mock tier.

## ARTICLE VIII — THE ATELIER LAW

**The law.** Character creation is a portrait-first atelier: the painted
hero large and central, repainting on tap rather than keystroke, with
structured appearance fields — hair, eyes, skin, build, attire, accessory —
each carrying die and pen under the Two Hands law with sovereign text
intact, each composing the sealed canon that feeds the anchor and the sheet.
The voice audition deals ten voices under the unchanged Tenor law, and
blessing one remains permanent.

**Enforcement point.** The atelier composes canon through the same sealed
fields the anchor and sheet mint from — what the player shapes is what the
painter is bound to.

**The gate.** The atelier gate (keyless, stage three); G33 proves the fields
land visibly on the portrait and the sheet, ten auditions deal, and one
blessing survives reload.

## OPERATIVE CLAUSES OF THE CUT

**The vertical clause.** Plates are vertical by default at one pinned
portrait ratio chosen from the provider's native set, subjects held toward
center; the ratio is checked deterministically on delivery, and the feed
frame letterboxes rather than crops, so a vertical plate renders whole on a
phone. Judged in G31 across the pinned viewport matrix.

**The easel priority clause.** The paint request enqueues the moment its cue
seals, ahead of voice and music; the queue order is deterministic and gated
by the easelPriority gate (keyless, stage one).

**The mended plate clause.** The production warden's second attempt may be a
targeted edit of the failed plate that preserves composition while removing
the offense; the proving harvest's mint ladders remain fresh-dice as pinned,
and no floor or bar moves.

**The quiet table clause.** The tool schema mirrors the validator's enums
exactly, beginning with the equip kinds, so the model is never taught to
write an operation the court refuses. Judged by the quietTable gate (stage
two) with fault injection: schema-validator lockstep, and the note-free
render contract proven in a full harvested session's DOM.

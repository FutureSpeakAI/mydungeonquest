SECTION 1: STAGE ONE, THE STORY SMITH
1. THE STORY SMITH LAW. On live keys, forging a world or a new volume may call a smith: a temperature-bounded model call returning a bespoke spine as strict JSON in exactly the shape the spine gates already check, acts, beats, deadlines, and a villain design fitted to the covenant and, for later volumes, to the carryover the saga law defines. Every candidate passes the existing spine schema court, the PG sweep, and the poisonSweep before it seats; a failing candidate is discarded and redrawn, never repaired by hand. A seated spine is minted once per volume, attested, and cached forever. The nine hand-built spines remain the keyless floor, the mock smith, and the known-good exemplars, so every gate still runs without keys and the free tier still gets whole stories.
2. The Two Hands govern the smith's door like everything else: a player may pick a shelf spine, roll a bespoke one, or open the deep forge; sovereign text stands.
Stage one gates, keyless, one: the smith gate (schema court on candidates, sweep rejection path, floor determinism, mint-once attestation, the mock smith deterministic). Pin rises P to P plus one.


SECTION 2: STAGE TWO, THE SAGA AND THE HEIR
1. THE SAGA LAW. Sealing a tale closes a volume, never a world. A saga manifest binds volumes in order, and each new volume's genesis cites the prior volume's head seal by hash, so the desk can walk a whole saga the way it walks a chain, refusing any break. The carryover is a pure fold, derived and cited, never destructive: the living cast, the places, the trove and purses, the standings, the open clocks, the open threads, and the calendar continue; the sealed volume stays sealed, exportable, and publishable exactly as before.
2. THE NEXT VOLUME is forged from what the last one left unpaid: the smith's carryover input names the open threads, open ambitions, and standings, so consequence is the seed of continuation, and the directive states the input contract.
3. THE HEIR LAW: when a hero dies or retires, a new hero may rise in the same world through the standing forge, inheriting the world's record whole while the fallen keep their memorials, their marks on the standings, and their unpaid debts as the new hero's inherited weather. Permadeath becomes a chapter break, and the Tenor and anchor laws apply to the heir untouched.
Stage two gates, keyless, two: the saga gate (the manifest, the citation chain verified and refused at the desk on a planted break, the carryover fold pure and cited, old single-volume tales loading untouched) and the heir gate (inheritance fold, memorial permanence, the forge path lawful mid-saga).

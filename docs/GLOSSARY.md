# GLOSSARY — The House Lexicon

One line per term, grouped by room. When code and directive disagree on a name, the code's name is listed with its directive alias — the record outranks the plan.

## At the table

- **Covenant** — the world's founding terms: tone, lines, veils. Absolute; even the sky defers to it.
- **Spine** — the story skeleton a world is forged on (e.g. `classic-epic`); **beats** are its stations; an **arc** is the tale's throughline.
- **Codex** — the living state: cast, regions, facts, blight, notes. Derived from the record, never from memory of the model.
- **Soul / card** — a character and the card that owns its canon: status, facts, bonds, voice, likeness.
- **Bond** — the closeness score between hero and soul; **blight** — the world's rising shadow, 0 and up.
- **Gloss** — the one-line truth a card wears (e.g. `Fell — …`).
- **The taste** — the free pour: exactly six turns.

## The record

- **The Quiet Record** — the append-only, hash-linked, device-signed journal. It *keeps*; the sermon retired.
- **Seal / envelope** — the act and the wrapper: every turn, tick, and event lands as a sealed envelope with a hash and a parent.
- **Chronicle** — an exported `.chronicle.json`; the offline verifier replays every hash, link, and signature.
- **Redaction / X-card** — player consent bending the visible story; the audit trail never bends.
- **The second proof** — every cut's directive ships in-repo, unedited, beside the code it built.

## The door

- **The One Door** — the model's single output channel: one forced `dm_turn` tool call, hostile until proven lawful.
- **Entropy** — eight pre-rolled dice per turn for world randomness, consumed in order, every use declared.
- **Repair turn / understudy / safe fallback** — the three-step mercy: exact violations back to the model once, then an OpenAI understudy under the same law, then inert prose. The game never advances on an invalid turn.
- **Context pack** — `[STATE] [STORY] [MEMORY] [ENTROPY] [RESOLUTION?] [PLAYER]`, assembled from the record under budget; the **scene floor** is the part never trimmed.

## The media wings

- **The Foundry** — the media works: paint and audio lanes with beat lookahead under stable cache keys.
- **Plate** — a painted still; **anchor** — the blessed reference a face answers to.
- **Binder's Door** — only attested media binds; provenance or nothing.
- **Audio Director** — the single throat every non-narration sound passes through; enforcer of the Sound Law.
- **Ensemble** — the locked pool of premade voices; **casting** reads the card and casts once, forever.
- **The Chronicler** — the retelling harness under three laws: no invention, no contradiction, retelling only.

## The house

- **The Floor** — keyless, deterministic, fully playable; the proving ground's native climate.
- **Toll-house** — where money lives; **refusal receipt** — a denial that arrives as a slip at the window, never a silent failure.
- **Watchtower** — the counters and ceilings that run before any provider is called.
- **Vault** — the house's copy of the record, chain-verified on deposit; **herald** — the house's messenger.
- **Keyless fork** — a clone with no keys and no house: full game, zero knowledge that money exists.

## The saga era (Directive V)

- **Saga** — one world across many tales; a **tale / volume** is a season within it.
- **Legacy packet** — what a sealing writes forward: souls with exact voices, locked canon, bonds, standing facts.
- **Interlude** — client-generated ticks bridging the years between volumes; never the model's to write.
- **Annal** — the code's name for Directive V's *memoir*: a Chronicler-lawful digest sealed at act's end, laddered into memory.
- **The ravens / What the Ravens Bring** — absence made story: deterministic offscreen batches and the return recap traced op-by-op.
- **Shared Sky / omen / sky note** — one seasonal sky over every world, entering the pack as a bounded hook, never a command.
- **Hearth / chair / unsent deed** — the record's home, a device sitting by it, and what a losing fork hands back instead of losing anything.
- **Direction tag** — per-line delivery notes derived from the record (`wounded`, `warm, close`) riding the speak call.
- **Minted voice** — a unique voice designed from a major soul's own card; cast once, kept forever.
- **Share card / shelf / fork** — the chapter's public face; the public shelf (secrets struck, quotes verbatim, text as text); a credited copy of covenant and genesis — never another patron's journal.
- **The Long Road** — the release this road leads to (fatescript 1.2.0) and the patron tier sized for years.
- **World Clock / span** — time derived by folding the record; a sealed `span` row covers roads and interludes; age walks the band ladder forward only, and the dead are outside time.
- **Trade op / ledger line** — double-entry, atomic, cause-bearing movement of coin and goods; wallets and inventories are projections of the record.
- **Witnessed price / price attack / the slate** — the first quote locks; uncaused change is refused by name; the slate reads trend and cause in one line.
- **Atlas / fog / reach / aftermath / missing** — placement phrases become coordinates in days of travel; the chart shows only the witnessed; a world event's zone is geometry; the world may move souls — `missing` — never end them.
- **Bearing / signature / roster** — the deterministic paint block derived from a card; the one trackable item the ledger moves and the paint follows; three painted subjects at most — speaker, villain, bond.
- **The Sitting / blessing / sheet** — three candidates of one identity, one accepted face, and only then the turnaround mints from the blessed anchor.
- **The Folio / marginalia** — the codex presented as the book the table keeps; every fact wears its turn seal in the margin, and the seal opens the record.
- **The Scriptorium / room law** — four specialized scribes (plot, character, setting, conflict) planning in notes and directives; the room plans, the door speaks (Agents' Room).
- **The Human Hand / tell families / counter-directive** — the court that measures the stated moral, the borrowed body, the tidy bow, and the hushed register in sealed narration, answering with capped, ordered directives (StoryScope).
- **The Salon / the human shelf** — the Tell Me A Story corpus, lawfully opened on your machine and never committed; the shelf the house calibrates against.

# EXPERIENCE-DIRECTIVE XIV — THE OPEN BOOK

*Ratified before any code, per the Series Preamble. TASK 58C, release
0.9.2. The codex earned its content across nine cuts; this directive
gives it a shape worthy of the record, and gives play a quiet table.*

The player's experience this directive protects: **everything the
record knows is one book away, shelved where a reader would look for
it; and the table where the tale is actually played holds only the
tale.** No fact moves house without its citation. No page costs a
token. No chart guesses.

---

## I. THE BOOK LAW

1. **Six chapters behind one button.** The codex becomes a book. The
   one door at the table (the nav button, now reading **Book**) opens
   it; inside, exactly six chapter doors stand in this order, a closed
   set:
   - **The Tale** — the chronicle and the recap.
   - **The People** — the soul pages as they stand.
   - **The Places** — the atlas and the Traveler's Chart.
   - **The Things** — the trove and the packs.
   - **The Debts** — the open threads.
   - **The Party** — the sheets and the packs' echoes.
2. **Derivation at zero token cost.** Every chapter is a pure fold over
   the sealed record — the same folds that earned their courts across
   nine cuts. Opening the book, walking its chapters, and reading every
   page spends nothing and calls no provider, ever.
3. **Every fact keeps its turn citation.** Existing pages move WITHOUT
   changing their proven content: the sworn-turn line on a thread, the
   hands-chain on a named thing, the discovered-turn cite on a place,
   the appearance timeline on a soul — byte-for-byte the surfaces the
   courts already pinned, reseated whole under their new chapter roofs.
4. **The head stays on every page.** The book's head (spine label, arc
   title, standing beat, the clock words, the day chip, the blight
   meter) stands above the chapter doors and is visible whatever
   chapter is open — the day chip's courts keep their target without
   walking.
5. **Navigation state survives the closing of the book within a
   sitting.** Chapter, open soul, open place, and open pack are held by
   the table, not by the book's own mount: close the book, reopen it,
   and it stands open to the same page. A new sitting opens the book
   fresh. Nothing of this state is sealed, synced, or exported.
6. **THE SURFACE MAP — no court's target is lost.** Every existing
   surface moves to exactly one chapter:

   | Existing surface (proven bytes) | Chapter | Standing courts |
   |---|---|---|
   | The shape of the tale (acts, walked beats, seal-tale row) | The Tale | G28b |
   | The evil design | The Tale | reveal law |
   | The scriptorium (room plans) | The Tale | copy law |
   | The human hand (tell court findings) | The Tale | copy law |
   | The cinematic archive (replay list) | The Tale | G12 replays |
   | The memoir — and the raven's recap while one stands | The Tale | G28b |
   | The cast grid and the soul pages | The People | G8, presence cut |
   | The region gallery and the place pages | The Places | G8, G20 |
   | THE TRAVELER'S CHART (new, Law II) | The Places | G28a |
   | The purse and the trove ledger | The Things | G19 |
   | THE PACKS (new, Law III) | The Things | G28c |
   | The open threads | The Debts | G8 |
   | The party strip and the companion sheets | The Party | G21, G23d |
   | Pack echoes per member (coin + held count, tapping through) | The Party | G28b |

   The book's head carries the day chip (G8, G14f). The hero's own
   sheet remains the sigil door's overlay, unmoved and unrenamed — its
   courts (G15 sheet sweep, the purse and chart ribbons) keep their
   addresses exactly.

## II. THE CHART LAW

1. **The world map is the Traveler's Chart, and it is honest: it draws
   only what the record knows.** The record does not guess, so neither
   does the chart.
2. **Medallions.** Every region the record holds renders as a medallion
   cut from its own sealed plate — the latest painted plate the store
   holds for that place, clipped to the disc. Where no plate has ever
   been sealed, the medallion stands plateless — the name on honest
   vellum — and no paint is commissioned to fill it. The chart
   introduces NO new paint, so the vision courts replay lawfully.
   Each medallion carries the place's name, its state word, and its
   discovery citation from the record's own fold.
3. **Roads.** A road exists between two regions ONLY where the sealed
   record proves the party traveled between them: a lawful change of
   standing ground, sealed by the scene operations under the ground
   law's own replay (struck rows prove nothing; a scene naming an
   unknown region is refused as the fold refuses it; a restatement of
   the standing ground moves nobody and draws nothing).
4. **The crossing's cost.** Each road is labeled with the days the
   crossing cost, derived from the calendar fold alone: the day
   standing at the arrival row, less the day standing at the row
   before it. A road crossed more than once carries each sealed
   crossing's cost in crossing order, identical costs spoken once.
   A crossing inside a single day says so plainly.
5. **The current ground is marked.** The chart marks where the party
   now stands, by the same replay the presence courts trust.
6. **The route is traceable.** The hero's route so far — every ground
   the record sealed, in played order — reads beneath the chart as a
   plain ordered line, each step cited.
7. **Deterministic layout.** Positions come from the atlas fold's own
   deterministic placement (locked anchors, hashed bearings for the
   unplaced) — the same tale always draws the same chart, byte-stable
   across rebuilds. No dice, no clock, no drift.
8. **Blank vellum.** Beyond the known regions the vellum stays blank.
   Nothing is sketched, hinted, or fogged-in where the record is
   silent.
9. **The chart is a door.** Tapping a medallion opens that place's own
   page in the book, in the Places chapter, with everything the place
   page already proves.

## III. THE PACK LAW

1. **A pack is a filtered view of the sealed trove, and says so.** The
   Things chapter presents the hero's ledger as built, and adds the
   pack view: one pack per party member — the hero first, then every
   standing member of the party fold — holdings from the trove's own
   hands-chain law, coin from the purse fold, nothing invented and no
   container system introduced. Each pack says in its own copy that it
   is the sealed trove, filtered to one bearer.
2. **Provenance one tap deep.** Tapping a holding opens its full
   hands-chain — every holder, since which turn, by what words — the
   trove's own citations, not a copy of them. Tapping a pack's coin
   opens the purse entries, each cited to its turn.
3. **Equipped slots wait for their own law.** Task 59 lands the
   equipment directive; this directive reserves the seat: the pack
   view's shape leaves room for equipped slots, and NOTHING in 58C
   fakes one. A pack in 0.9.2 has holdings and coin, nothing more.

## IV. THE TABLE LAW

1. **The play surface carries the feed, the composer, the dice, and
   exactly four chips.** The chips are a CLOSED set; adding a fifth
   chip requires amending this directive:
   - **The day and its watch** — from the calendar fold's own words.
   - **The ground** — the standing scene by the presence replay; before
     any scene stands, the chip says the tale has not yet set one.
   - **The party's faces** — the party fold's members; the hero travels
     alone when it is empty, and the chip says so.
   - **The hero's health** — hit points, nothing else.
2. **Everything else lives in the book.** Armor class, coin, experience,
   and the blight meter leave the table: the sheet and the book carry
   them where their courts already look. A door is not a chip: the
   sigil door (the hero's sheet), the Hearth, the Book, Care, and the
   wax seal remain doors, and no door carries status copy.
3. **The initiative tracker appears only while combat stands** — the
   sealed combat state's own flag — and leaves when it ends. Outside
   combat the table holds no trace of it.
4. **Reduced motion is honored on every new surface.** The standing
   stillness law (the settings toggle OR the device's own
   prefers-reduced-motion) reaches the book, the chart, the packs, and
   the chips: under stillness, nothing on these surfaces animates.

## V. GATES AND CRITERIA

1. **Keyless gates grow by exactly three.** Pin rises 116 → 119.
   - **The book gate** (`evals/book.test.mjs`) — six chapter doors
     exactly (a closed set); every existing proven surface reachable at
     its mapped chapter; the citation rendering law intact on the moved
     pages; the book a controlled surface (the table owns its
     navigation state).
   - **The chart gate** (`evals/travelersChart.test.mjs`) — medallions
     from the record's regions only; roads only where travel is sealed,
     with day labels from the calendar fold; the refusals (struck rows,
     unknown regions, restatements) prove nothing; deterministic layout
     byte-stable across rebuilds; the current ground and the played
     route; blank vellum where the record is silent.
   - **The table gate** (`evals/tableLaw.test.mjs`) — the closed chip
     set, exactly four, by name; chip contents from the folds alone;
     the tracker's flag bound to the sealed combat state; the stillness
     law's reach.
2. **The proving loop gains G28 THE BOOK** (project `g28-book`), the
   fixture court:
   - **G28a THE CHART** — the seeded fixture's chart shows its regions
     as medallions; exactly ONE road, carrying the fixture's known
     crossing cost from the calendar fold; no road anywhere the record
     holds no travel; the current ground marked; the route in played
     order; tapping a medallion opens that place's page.
   - **G28b THE BOOK** — each chapter opens and its known fixture facts
     render with citations; navigation state survives closing and
     reopening the book within the sitting.
   - **G28c THE PACKS** — the pack view shows a party member's holding

   - **G28d THE TABLE** — the four chips and only the four chips; no

3. **The re-proving law.** Every prior DOM criterion — G8, G14, G19,
   G20, G21, and G23 — is re-proven at the new addresses in the same
   run: the courts keep their assertions byte-for-byte and gain only
   the navigation to reach their moved targets. A navigation edit is a
   sanctioned test edit, logged in LOOP_LOG.md, and weakens nothing.
4. **The copy law covers every new surface.** The G15 sweeps walk all
   six chapters, the chart, the packs, and the chips.
5. **No new paint.** The chart cuts medallions from plates already
   sealed in the store; nothing in 58C commissions, regenerates, or
   retouches a plate. The vision courts' stores replay untouched.
